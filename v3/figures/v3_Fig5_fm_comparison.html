Figure 5. Foundation Model Comparison for Spaceflight Detection
PCA-LR baseline vs four foundation models across 7 tissues. LOMO cross-validation (multi-mission) or 5-fold stratified CV (single-mission).
A. AUROC by Model and Tissue
Grouped bars: PCA-LR gene-level baseline (gray), Geneformer (orange), scGPT (blue), UCE-4L (green), scFoundation (purple). Dashed line = chance (0.5). Stars indicate permutation p < 0.05.
B. Foundation Model AUROC with 95% CI
Forest plot for UCE (green) and scFoundation (purple). Only 5 multi-mission tissues shown (CI requires bootstrap). Filled = p < 0.05.
C. Numerical Summary
AUROC values with statistical significance. Bold = best FM per tissue. Blue = p < 0.05.
Key finding: PCA-LR (simple variance filter + PCA + logistic regression) outperforms all four foundation models on most tissues. scFoundation is the best FM overall (liver 0.635**, gastro 0.691*), but still below PCA-LR (liver 0.670, gastro 0.824). UCE shows moderate performance on thymus (0.632*) but is hampered by stochastic gene sampling (patched with seed=42). Geneformer and scGPT (v1 results, fine-tuned) also underperform PCA-LR.

Interpretation: Pre-trained cell atlas representations (healthy tissue variation) do not capture spaceflight perturbation signals. Spaceflight detection requires mission-specific transcriptomic shifts that are better captured by direct statistical features (variance-filtered gene expression → PCA) than by learned biological priors from single-cell atlases. The FM embedding space encodes cell identity, not environmental perturbation.
