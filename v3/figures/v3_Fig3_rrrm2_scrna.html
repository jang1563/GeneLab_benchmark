GLDS-402/403/404/405 — 4 tissues (PBMC, spleen, femur BM, humerus BM), 8 FLT + 8 GC animals, PCA(50)-LR LOAO classifier
A. PBMC Cell-Type Classification (F5c)
LOAO AUROC per cell type, bootstrap CI (n=2000), permutation p (n=5000)
B. Cross-Tissue Cell-Type AUROC (F5c/F5e)
Best cell types per tissue. Bone marrow = all near chance
C. Cross-Mission Concordance: RRRM-1 ↔ RRRM-2 (F5d)
Hallmark NES Spearman r (40–42 common pathways). Dashed: E1 bulk baseline r=0.352
D. Bone Marrow Cell-Type Benchmark (F5e)
Femur vs humerus AUROC per cell type (14 cell types). All near chance
Interpretation: RRRM-2 PBMC shows strong spaceflight signal in NK cells (AUROC=0.845***) and T cells (0.752***), consistent with known immune activation in spaceflight. Spleen shows modest signal (B cell 0.562***, macrophage 0.521***). Bone marrow is a genuine negative: all 14 cell types across both anatomical sites (femur/humerus) yield AUROC 0.27–0.54, suggesting hematopoietic progenitors are buffered from spaceflight effects at the transcriptomic level. Cross-mission concordance (RRRM-1 ↔ RRRM-2) is low: no cell type exceeds the E1 bulk baseline (r=0.352). The strongest signal is erythroid (r=−0.362*, p=0.018), which is negatively correlated, likely reflecting gene set naming differences between mh.all GMT (RRRM-1) and Enrichr Hallmark (RRRM-2).
