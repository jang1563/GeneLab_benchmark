Phase 4 (A7/A7b/A8) new tissue classification + Phase 5 (R1–R3) radiation vs HLU ground analog DGE concordance
A. New Tissue Spaceflight Classification (A7/A7b)
PCA-LR, 5-fold stratified CV, RR-6 mission. Bootstrap CI (n=2000), permutation p (n=5000)
B. Extended Skin LOMO Across 6 Missions (A8)
Leave-one-mission-out CV. Color by strain. Mean AUROC = 0.669
C. Radiation vs ISS DGE Concordance (R2/R3)
Spearman r of log2FC: ground radiation/HLU/combined vs ISS flight (skin, GLDS-237)
D. Ground Analog Classification (R1)
PCA-LR per contrast (radiation/HLU/combined vs control), 3 tissues (GLDS-202/211/237)
Interpretation: New tissue extensions reveal variable spaceflight detection: colon achieves AUROC=0.900 (marginal p=0.07, strongest new tissue), while lung is modest at 0.608 (ns). Extended skin LOMO shows strong strain dependence: C57BL/6J missions (MHU-2, RR-7) achieve 0.89–0.97***, while BAL-TAL (RR-5) drops to 0.31–0.38, confirming that mouse strain is a critical confound for cross-mission generalization. Radiation analysis (R2/R3) reveals a striking pattern: radiation and HLU have opposite-signed correlations with ISS flight (radiation: r=+0.14 to +0.44; HLU: r=−0.20 to −0.50), and their combination nearly cancels out (r≈−0.03). This suggests ISS spaceflight effects are not simply additive radiation + microgravity, but involve complex interactions between these stressors.
