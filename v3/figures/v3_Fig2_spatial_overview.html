Figure 2. OSD-352 RR-3 Brain Visium Spatial Transcriptomics Benchmark
Masarapu et al. 2024 (Nature Communications) — 3 FLT + 3 GC animals, 12 sections, 24,085 spots
A. Classification AUROC (F3a/F3d)
PCA(≤5)-LR, LOAO 6-fold, exact permutation test (C(6,3)=20)
All AUROCs below chance — brain tissue shows no detectable spaceflight signal at n=6
B. Spatially Variable Genes (F3b)
Moran's I per section (k=6 neighbors), 2000 HVGs tested
C. Section Quality Metrics
Spots per section by condition and slide
D. SVG Condition Specificity
FLT-specific vs GC-specific spatially variable genes
Interpretation: Brain tissue from RR-3 shows minimal spaceflight transcriptomic response. Section-level pseudo-bulk AUROC = 0.139 and companion bulk AUROC = 0.000 (systematic inversion with n=6 LOO-CV). Only 1 DEG at padj<0.05 in companion bulk RNA-seq. SVG analysis shows modest FLT enrichment (d=0.70, p=0.240) but high Jaccard overlap (0.805) indicates brain spatial architecture dominates over condition-specific spatial patterns. The 139 FLT-specific SVGs (vs 6 GC-specific) warrant further investigation but may reflect technical quality differences between capture areas. F3c (region-specific AUROC) was not computed due to corrupted Seurat metadata file on OSDR. This negative result is informative: not all tissues respond equally to spaceflight at current sample sizes.
