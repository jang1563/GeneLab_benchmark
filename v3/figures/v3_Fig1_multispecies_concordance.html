Figure 1. Multi-Species KEGG Pathway NES Concordance
Mouse (6 tissues) × Drosophila (head, GLDS-207) — fGSEA KEGG NES, Spearman correlation with permutation p (n=5000)
A. Pairwise NES Concordance Heatmap (E4)
Spearman r of KEGG pathway NES between all tissue/species pairs. 221–295 common pathways per pair. * p<0.05, ** p<0.01, *** p<0.001
B. Cross-Species Concordance: Drosophila ↔ Mouse
Spearman r of KEGG NES (222 pathways, FBgn→human orthologs, 31.2% coverage)
C. Mouse Intra-Species Top Concordances
Strongest positive and negative tissue pair correlations (KEGG NES)
Interpretation: Cross-species KEGG pathway concordance reveals tissue-specific patterns of spaceflight response conservation. Drosophila head shows significant negative correlation with mouse skin (r=−0.586***), thymus (r=−0.188**), and gastrocnemius (r=−0.180**), suggesting divergent or opposing pathway regulation across species for these tissues. Mouse intra-species concordance ranges from r=+0.324*** (eye↔liver) to r=−0.267*** (kidney↔skin), confirming highly tissue-specific spaceflight transcriptomic responses even within a single species. Arabidopsis cross-kingdom comparison yielded 0 orthologs (expected for plant↔animal), confirming the kingdom boundary for ortholog-based NES concordance. Key finding: spaceflight pathway responses are not uniformly conserved — tissue and species context dominate.
